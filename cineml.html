CINE-ML

As the continuum for CINEPATH, the research-based art project I started in 2018 investigating film locations, CineML takes the machine learning approach to analyze cinema, in relation to places, as well as the digital-analog conversion looking into the real and unreal.



PARIS

CineML: Paris (2021) keeps focusing on the relationship between locations in films and places in real life through training generative adversarial networks models, and building illusions based on a shared mentality on Instagram.





THE PERFECT HUMAN

Through the process of training with StyleGAN2, I look into a 13-min short film in Black and White The Perfect Human directed by JÃ¸rgen Leth in 1967 and re-examine the idea of Perfect Human that was previous portrayed as a middle class Danish couple performing everyday rituals.

README

To some extent, every film is stagged. The original film of The Perfect Human by Leth was too obviously staged in a boundless space. It is an unrealistic environment to portray the non-existing perfect human. White background successfully situates the concept of perfect human far from the reality. And the reason I intentionally choose the 360p low-resolution video found on YouTube and train a neural network based on it is to create an ambiguous and abstract remake that complicates the contested notion of perfect human. The computer-generated fakes also played a crucial part of generalizing what perfect human means.
