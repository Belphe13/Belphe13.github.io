GRAINGER ENGINEERING ▼

Throughout my time at Siebel Center for Design, I have dedicated my work to understand and improve engineering student experience and college perceptions through the SCD taxonomy. My team role shifted from design researcher to design strategist where I got the chance to co-lead undergraduate interns and go through the whole process from researching planning to the final presentation at a rapid pace in two months as response to moving education online.

Here I will briefly go over this research project related to Grainger student experience and engagement amid the pandemic.


RESPONSIBILITIES

- Co-led the project team within a group of 5
- Met with stakeholders and proposed research plan
- Conducted 10+ student interviews and co-creation sessions
- Synthesized interview findings and present to Grainger Engineering leadership team
- Compiled a quick guide for quality remote instructions


TIMELINE



SCD has been tasked to use a human-centered approach to explore and understand the options for creating engagement opportunities at Grainger College of Engineering in a COVID-19 world.

We drafted a research proposal that presented to stakeholders and quickly dived into the user research recruitment. We conducted one-hour-long interview sessions with students that have experience online learning to get the student perspectives. And we identified several opportunity areas and shared them back to participants and improved based on student feedbacks.


DELIVERABLES

We had one research readout amid the research process as well as the final presentation to the leadership team. We also collaborated with our graphic design team to create a guideline for remote instructions and disseminated our research findings for internal use.
