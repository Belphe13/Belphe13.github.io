STUDENT EXPERIENCE


In response to the global pandemic and in anticipation of its lasting effects, it will be vitally important to re-imagine how Grainger College of Engineering might offer meaningful engagement opportunities for students to interact with their peers, instructors, and the larger UIUC community. This project uses human-centered design research to assess student and instructor needs and desires in building community both inside and outside of the classroom and to identify and implement adaptable and scalable programs/tools/experiences in Fall 2020 and beyond.


PROBLEMS

  • What have other institutions implemented to create community and engagement during this period of alternative instruction? What has been successful and what has been a failure?
  • What does the current engagement landscape look like for Grainger students? For Grainger faculty and TAs?
  • How might elements of the current engagement landscape be translated to a virtual environment? What new tools, experiences, or supports need to be created?
  • What tools or processes have worked, and what have not? Where are there opportunities for improvement?
  • What are the minimum viable improvements that can be rapidly deployed for Fall 2020?
  • How might these improvements be adapted or scaled to meet the needs of all Grainger students?
  • How can Grainger communicate these improvements to students who have concerns about enrolling in the Fall?

RESPONSIBILITIES

  • Co-led the project team within a group of 5
  • Met with stakeholders and proposed research plan
  • Conducted 10+ student interviews and co-creation sessions
  • Synthesized interview findings and present to Grainger Engineering leadership team
  • Compiled a quick guide for quality remote instructions

RESEARCH PLAN



The research team drafted a proposal that was presented to stakeholders and quickly dived into the user research recruitment. I conducted 12 30-minute-long interview and feedback sessions with students that have experience online learning to get the student perspectives. And I identified several opportunity areas and shared them back to participants for further feedback.


RESEARCH FINDINGS

Our initial insights are broken down into 3 categories: academic, social and personal.


Academic:

  1. Remote education can limit student opportunities and comfortability with engaging with instructors.
  2. Remote education can limit access to the hands-on experience students want.

Social:

  1. Virtual education can make it more difficult to find and form study groups.
  2. Students go to events to meet new people or to engage with the small group they came with, both difficult to recreate virtually.
  3. On campus, connections are often a result of daily interstitial interactions.

Personal:

  1. Remote education can make structuring time more difficult for students.
  2. Many students' productivity is strongly influenced by space association.
  3. Students seem to miss the moments that remind them of the passage of time.


DELIVERABLES
